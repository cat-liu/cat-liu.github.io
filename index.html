My name is Cathy and I’m savvy in a wide variety of disciplines. Throughout my career, my managers and peers have thought of me as a “swiss-army knife”.

My most recent journey was co-founding Buji, a consumer marketplace startup. We learned the stories of more than 250 local merchants and helped them connect with their audiences and build their storefronts through livestream video.

Before Buji, my path was shaped by diverse interests; such as, immersing myself in tech cultures abroad. I spent some time in Shanghai in the product team at Didi where I dove deep into China's car rental scene. In partnership with Avis, we worked with engineering to launch a car rental product to 175 countries internationally. My curiosity about supply-chain operations grew during my time at UC San Diego, where I studied Management Science with a secondary in Cognitive Science. This path led me to business operations at Intel where I was entrusted with overseeing the growth of 18 client and data center product lines.

Beyond my professional endeavors, I find great fulfillment in leveraging my skills to help others. I had the unique opportunity to collaborate with Sam Altman and ship product design for the open source project, helpwithcovid.com.

While I am not working, I enjoy traveling around the world, working on my wellness in pilates classes, learning about film photography, discovering lesser known musicians, and indulging in provocative Netflix documentaries.

I love consumer marketplaces and am curious about adjacent sectors that could transform the space, such as fintech, AI, social media, and logistics. I am currently located in SF Bay Area. I am open to chatting about new ideas, please reach out!
